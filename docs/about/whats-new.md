# What's new

## August 2025

Hello, dear readers! August has been a busy month for us at the Fabric Adoption Framework, and I’m excited to share some of the changes we’ve made to enhance your experience. We’ve added a new License section to our documentation, which is now easily accessible through the navigation menu. This addition is important because it clarifies the terms under which our materials can be used, ensuring you have all the information you need to navigate our resources confidently (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-08-2fcc4ca958e093e082d40a93ab14ba804b31822d.md).

In our efforts to streamline your navigation, we’ve also collapsed sidebar categories for clarity. This change aims to make it easier for you to find what you’re looking for without feeling overwhelmed by options (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-07-428a3d2df5e70d8c5e77d1111e8edbf72439302d.md). Additionally, we’ve synchronized our documentation with the latest strategy methodology documents, ensuring that the content you’re accessing is up-to-date and relevant (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-08-f359f7af9238c768ef15da3bd9b4a18af45e0d78.md).

These updates reflect our commitment to providing you with a user-friendly experience as you navigate the world of Fabric adoption. I want to extend my heartfelt thanks to everyone who contributed to these changes; your efforts truly make a difference in our community. I hope these enhancements make your journey with us even more enjoyable! (531 commits)

## July 2025

Hello, dear readers! I’m excited to share some significant updates from July that I believe will enhance your experience with the Fabric Adoption Framework. This month, we’ve made strides in establishing the initial content for our adoption framework, which is designed to guide you through the process of integrating our solutions into your projects. You can explore the details of this new content in our [initial documentation](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-07-20-b6ea8bd71edcd6fcab2d774df9ea7b7b415bcbc2.md), as well as in our [overview](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-07-20-21d18ea99b20ea417847b8f13538bf12ad8d7505.md).

Why does this matter? Well, having clear and structured documentation is essential for you to navigate the complexities of adoption with confidence. By providing a comprehensive overview and detailed guidance, we aim to empower you to make informed decisions and feel supported every step of the way. We’ve also removed some initial documentation that no longer aligns with our vision, ensuring that you have access to the most relevant and helpful information (you can read more about that [here](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-07-20-3948fa7bc9ab671af8690e6527e831adebbec1dc.md)).

I want to extend a heartfelt thank you to everyone who contributed to these changes. Your efforts are invaluable in shaping a resource that truly meets the needs of our community. As we continue to refine and expand our documentation, I’m optimistic that these updates will make a positive impact on your adoption journey. Together, we’re building something special! (Read more about all the changes this month in [115 commits](https://github.com/TheTrustedAdvisor/FabricAdoptionFramework/commits/main?since=2025-07-01&until=2025-07-31)).

## June 2025

Hello, dear readers! This month, we made a significant change to our documentation structure by removing the "What's New" section. This decision was made to streamline our content and make it easier for you to find the information you need without sifting through updates that may no longer serve you. I truly believe this will enhance your navigation experience and help you focus on the core resources that matter most to your journey with the Fabric Adoption Framework. If you're curious about the specifics, you can read more about it [here](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-06-03-5a7d4f72ccbbd73c700b77c1b485216d1e29c0ea.md).

This change matters because it reflects our commitment to providing you with a clear and concise documentation experience. By removing outdated sections, we hope to reduce clutter and allow you to engage more deeply with the content that truly supports your needs. I appreciate the feedback from our community that guided us in this direction, and I'm excited to see how this will positively impact your experience.

As we continue to evolve, your insights remain invaluable. Thank you for being a part of this journey with us! I look forward to hearing how these changes resonate with you. Together, we can make the Fabric Adoption Framework an even more welcoming and effective resource for everyone.
