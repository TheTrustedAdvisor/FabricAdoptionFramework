# What's new

## August 2025

Hello dear readers! August has been a bustling month for the Fabric Adoption Framework, and I’m excited to share some of the thoughtful changes we've made to enhance your experience. One of the most significant updates is the addition of a License section to our documentation, which now includes a direct link in the navigation menu. This change not only clarifies how you can use our resources but also reinforces our commitment to transparency and accessibility (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-08-2fcc4ca958e093e082d40a93ab14ba804b31822d.md). 

We’ve also taken steps to streamline our documentation by collapsing sidebar categories for better clarity. This means you can navigate through our content more easily, making it simpler to find exactly what you need without feeling overwhelmed (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-07-428a3d2df5e70d8c5e77d1111e8edbf72439302d.md). Additionally, we’ve synchronized our published strategy methodology documents, ensuring that you have the most current and relevant information at your fingertips (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-08-f359f7af9238c768ef15da3bd9b4a18af45e0d78.md). 

These updates are all about making your journey with us smoother and more intuitive. I want to extend a heartfelt thank you to our contributors and the community for their ongoing support and feedback, which make these improvements possible. Together, we’re building a more robust framework for everyone involved. (531 commits)

## July 2025

Hello, dear readers! I’m excited to share some wonderful updates from July 2025 that I believe will enhance your experience with the Fabric Adoption Framework. This month, we’ve made significant strides in our documentation by introducing the initial content for the adoption framework. This new material is designed to guide you through the adoption process more effectively, providing clarity and support as you navigate your journey. You can check out the details of this content rollout [here](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-07-20-b6ea8bd71edcd6fcab2d774df9ea7b7b415bcbc2.md).

In addition to adding new content, we also made the decision to remove some initial documentation files that were no longer aligned with our framework's goals. This streamlining is essential for ensuring that you have access to the most relevant and helpful resources. I truly believe that these changes will make your navigation smoother and your learning experience richer. If you’re curious about the specifics, you can find more information on the removals [here](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-07-20-3948fa7bc9ab671af8690e6527e831adebbec1dc.md).

I want to take a moment to thank everyone who contributed to these updates. Your feedback and input are invaluable in shaping our documentation to better serve the community. I hope you find the new resources helpful and inspiring as you embark on your adoption journey. Together, we’re making great progress! ## June 2025

Hello, dear readers! This June, we made some significant changes to our documentation that I believe will enhance your experience with the Fabric Adoption Framework. We decided to remove the "What's New" documentation section. This change was made to streamline our content and ensure that you can find the most relevant information without any clutter. If you're curious about the details of this decision, you can read more about it [here](https://www.fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-06-03-5a7d4f72ccbbd73c700b77c1b485216d1e29c0ea.md).

This adjustment matters because it allows us to focus on providing clearer and more concise content, making it easier for you to navigate and find what you need. By removing this section, we aim to create a more cohesive experience that reflects the evolving nature of our documentation. We hope this will help you engage more deeply with the material and feel more connected to our community.

I want to take a moment to thank everyone who contributed to this decision. Your feedback and insights are invaluable, and they help us shape a better resource for all users. We’re excited about the positive impact these changes will have, and we can’t wait to hear your thoughts!
