# What's new

## August 2025

Hello, dear readers! I’m excited to share some wonderful updates from August 2025 that I believe will enhance your experience with the Fabric Adoption Framework. This month, we’ve made significant strides in refining our documentation, including the addition of a new License section, which you can now easily find in our navigation menu. This change not only clarifies our usage guidelines but also fosters transparency, making it easier for you to understand how to engage with our materials (Read more ›).

We’ve also taken steps to improve the overall organization of our documentation. By collapsing sidebar categories, we’ve aimed to create a cleaner and more intuitive navigation experience. This should help you find what you need more quickly and efficiently, allowing you to focus on what truly matters: your journey with Fabric adoption (Read more ›). Additionally, we’ve synchronized our published strategies and methodology documents, ensuring that you have access to the most relevant and up-to-date information (Read more ›).

A heartfelt thank you goes out to our contributors and the community for your ongoing support and input. Your engagement helps us create a more user-friendly environment, and I’m hopeful that these changes will make a positive impact on your experience with our resources. Together, we’re building a valuable foundation for everyone involved in the Fabric Adoption Framework. ## June 2025

Hello, dear readers! I’m excited to share some important updates from June 2025 that will enhance your experience on the Fabric Adoption Framework website. This month, we made the decision to remove the "What's New" documentation section. While it may seem like a small change, it reflects our commitment to streamline the content and ensure that you can find the most relevant information without any distractions. You can read more about this decision [here](https://www.fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-06-03-5a7d4f72ccbbd73c700b77c1b485216d1e29c0ea.md).

By simplifying our navigation and focusing on the most essential content, we hope to create a more intuitive experience for you. This change allows us to direct our energy towards enhancing other areas of the documentation that truly matter to your journey with the framework. We believe that this will make it easier for you to access the information you need, helping you to adopt and implement the framework more effectively. For more details, check out the full discussion [here](https://www.fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-06-03-3a35144aa92198574f983f6a3054dd2b5db3ab9c.md).

I want to take a moment to thank our contributors and the community for their ongoing support and feedback. Your insights have been invaluable in shaping our documentation, and I’m confident that these changes will lead to a more enjoyable and productive experience for everyone. As we continue to evolve, I look forward to hearing your thoughts on these updates and any suggestions you may have for further improvements. Together, we can make this framework even better!
