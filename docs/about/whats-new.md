Hello, dear readers! August has been a bustling month for the Fabric Adoption Framework, and I’m excited to share some of the changes that have taken place to enhance your experience with our documentation. We’ve synchronized new content, including initial project setup and methodology documents, which means you’ll find clearer pathways and resources to guide you through your adoption journey (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-08-29aca670872315962827412318cbc8e797896e58.md). For instance, we added a License section to our documentation and included it in the navigation menu, making it easier for you to access important information about usage rights (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-08-2fcc4ca958e093e082d40a93ab14ba804b31822d.md). These updates are all about creating a smoother and more intuitive experience for you, our valued community. I want to extend a heartfelt thank you to everyone who contributed to these changes; your efforts make a real difference. I hope you enjoy exploring the refreshed content and structure as much as we enjoyed putting it together. ## June 2025

Hello, dear readers! This month, we've made some significant changes to the Fabric Adoption Framework documentation that I’m excited to share with you. Most notably, we've removed the "What's New" section from our documentation. This decision was made to streamline your experience and make it easier for you to find the most relevant information without sifting through updates that may not directly impact your journey with us. You can read more about this change [here](https://www.fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-06-03-5a7d4f72ccbbd73c700b77c1b485216d1e29c0ea.md).

I believe this adjustment matters because it reflects our commitment to providing you with a cleaner, more intuitive navigation experience. By eliminating redundancy, we hope to enhance your ability to focus on the core content that truly supports your adoption of our framework. This is all part of our ongoing effort to ensure that your interaction with the documentation is as smooth and enjoyable as possible.

I want to take a moment to thank our contributors and the community for their valuable feedback, which guided us in making these changes. Your insights are crucial in shaping a documentation experience that serves you best.
