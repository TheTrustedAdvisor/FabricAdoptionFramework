# What's new

## August 2025

Hello, dear readers! I’m excited to share some lovely updates from August that I believe will enhance your experience with the Fabric Adoption Framework. We’ve made several changes to our documentation that aim to make it more accessible and user-friendly. For instance, we’ve added a new License section to the documentation, along with a link in the navigation menu to help you easily find this important information (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-08-2fcc4ca958e093e082d40a93ab14ba804b31822d.md). This addition not only clarifies the usage rights but also reflects our commitment to transparency.

In our ongoing effort to streamline your navigation, we’ve collapsed sidebar categories for better clarity, making it easier for you to find what you need without feeling overwhelmed (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-07-428a3d2df5e70d8c5e77d1111e8edbf72439302d.md). We also synchronized various strategy documents to ensure you have the most up-to-date resources at your fingertips (Read more › /about/changes/2025-08-08-9993fe2ef400b1868a1874a379e6fe864b68fd0c.md). 

These updates matter because they enhance your journey with us, making it smoother and more intuitive. I want to extend my heartfelt thanks to all the contributors who helped bring these changes to life. Your efforts truly make a difference, and I look forward to seeing how these improvements impact your experience! (531 commits)

## July 2025

Hello, dear readers! July has been a transformative month for the Fabric Adoption Framework, and I’m excited to share some of the wonderful changes we’ve made to enhance your experience. We’ve rolled out initial content for the adoption framework, which lays the groundwork for understanding how to effectively implement our practices. This content is designed to be user-friendly and approachable, making it easier for you to navigate the framework and find the information you need. You can check out the details about this new content [here](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-07-20-b6ea8bd71edcd6fcab2d774df9ea7b7b415bcbc2.md).

In addition to adding new content, we’ve also made the decision to remove some initial documentation that was no longer relevant. This cleanup allows us to streamline your experience and focus on the most pertinent information. By decluttering our resources, we hope to make it easier for you to find what you’re looking for without sifting through outdated material. You can read more about the removals [here](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-07-20-3948fa7bc9ab671af8690e6527e831adebbec1dc.md).

These updates are a direct response to your feedback and our commitment to providing a more cohesive and user-centered experience. I truly appreciate the contributions from our community that have helped shape these changes, and I’m looking forward to seeing how they positively impact your journey with the Fabric Adoption Framework. Together, we’re building something special! (Read more ›) [115 commits](https://github.com/TheTrustedAdvisor/FabricAdoptionFramework/commits/main?since=2025-07-01&until=2025-07-31)

## June 2025

Hello, dear readers! This month, we made a significant change to our documentation structure that I believe will enhance your experience. We decided to remove the "What's New" section from our documentation. While this section has served us well in keeping you informed, we felt that it was time to streamline our content and make it easier for you to navigate through the information that truly matters. You can read more about this decision in detail here: [Read more ›](https://fabricadoptionframework.com/about/changes/2025-06-03-5a7d4f72ccbbd73c700b77c1b485216d1e29c0ea.md).

Why does this matter? By removing the "What's New" section, we aim to create a more cohesive and user-friendly experience. We want you to spend less time searching for updates and more time engaging with the resources that will help you in your journey. This change reflects our commitment to continuously improve our documentation based on your feedback and needs. I truly appreciate the contributions from our community that have guided us in making this decision.

As we move forward, I expect this adjustment will lead to a more seamless navigation experience for all of you. Your insights are invaluable, and I encourage you to keep sharing your thoughts with us. Together, we can continue to refine our documentation and make it a place where you feel supported and empowered. Thank you for being a part of our community!
